Foundation

The Mindset Shift

One of the first things I tell junior engineers is this: compliance is a floor, not a ceiling. You can be fully PCI DSS compliant and still get breached. You can pass a HIPAA audit and still expose patient data. Compliance frameworks define the minimum expected controls — good security architecture defines what actually protects the business.

Core Principle
Both frameworks converge on the same fundamentals: encrypt everything, log everything, control access tightly, and be able to prove it. Get that right and you'll be dangerous in any compliance conversation.

The goal of this guide is to understand why each control exists, how it maps to real AWS architecture, and how to talk about it fluently in an interview or audit.

PCI DSS

What Is PCI DSS?

PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) is an industry-driven security standard — not a law — created and maintained by the PCI Security Standards Council (PCI SSC). It was founded by Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, and JCB. Current version: PCI DSS v4.0 (v3.2.1 retired March 2024).

The standard applies to any organization that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data (CHD) or sensitive authentication data (SAD).

CHD vs SAD — The Critical Distinction

This is a top interview trap. Know exactly what can and cannot be stored post-authorization:

Data ElementCategoryExamplesPost-Auth Storage
Primary Account Number (PAN)CHD16-digit card numberYes — must be rendered unreadable
Cardholder NameCHDName on cardYes
Service CodeCHD3-digit on mag stripeYes
Expiration DateCHDMM/YYYes
Full Mag Stripe DataSADTrack 1 / Track 2NEVER
CAV2 / CVV2 / CVC2SAD3–4 digit security codeNEVER
PIN / PIN BlockSADATM PINNEVER
Interview Answer
"SAD — Sensitive Authentication Data — can never be stored after authorization completes, regardless of encryption. The mag stripe, CVV, and PIN are absolute restrictions in PCI DSS. CHD elements like PAN can be stored but must be rendered unreadable via tokenization, truncation, or encryption."

PCI DSS

Scoping & the CDE

Scoping is the process of identifying every system component that must comply with PCI DSS. In enterprise environments, scope reduction is everything. Every system in scope must meet all 12 requirements.

Scope CategoryDefinition
In-scope (CDE)All systems that store, process, or transmit CHD/SAD
Connected-toSystems that can reach the CDE — also in scope unless properly isolated
Out-of-scopeSystems with no path to the CDE and cannot impact its security
Critical Point
If your HR system can talk to your payment processor, your HR system is now in PCI scope. Network segmentation is foundational — not just a Req 1 checkbox.

Tokenization — The King of Scope Reduction

Tokenization replaces the PAN with a surrogate value (token) that has no mathematical relationship to the original PAN. Unlike encryption (reversible with the key), a token is meaningless without access to the token vault.

In practice: the customer enters their card number once, the payment service exchanges it for a token with a provider (Stripe, Braintree, or an HSM-backed vault), and every downstream system sees only the token. Your order management, CRM, and analytics systems never touch PAN — they're entirely out of PCI scope.

MechanismDescriptionScope Impact
TokenizationNon-reversible surrogate; requires vault to map backRemoves PAN from downstream systems entirely
P2PE (Validated)PCI SSC-validated; encrypted at point of interaction through to acquirerCan dramatically reduce/eliminate merchant CDE
E2EE (Generic)End-to-end encryption, not PCI-validatedReduces scope but less credit from QSA than P2PE
TruncationStore only first 6 / last 4 digits of PANStored value no longer PAN — out of scope

HIPAA

What Is HIPAA?

HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996) is a U.S. federal law enforced by the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS), specifically the Office for Civil Rights (OCR). Unlike PCI DSS, there's no certification body — you don't "pass" HIPAA. You demonstrate an ongoing, risk-based compliance posture.

Entity TypeDefinitionExamples
Covered Entity (CE)Organizations directly subject to HIPAAHospitals, health plans, clearinghouses
Business Associate (BA)Third parties that handle PHI on behalf of CEsAWS, EHR vendors, billing companies
SubcontractorA BA's vendor who also touches PHIAWS sub-processors — also need BAA

The Three HIPAA Rules

1. Privacy Rule — Establishes standards for who can access and use PHI. Defines patient rights. Requires a Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP). Governs disclosures and the Minimum Necessary Standard.

2. Security Rule — Focuses specifically on ePHI and defines three categories of safeguards (see below). Uses the terms Required and Addressable for implementation specifications.

3. Breach Notification Rule — Requires notification when unsecured PHI is impermissibly used or disclosed. Timelines are firm and legally binding.

Security Rule Safeguards

Safeguard TypeExamples
AdministrativeRisk analysis, risk management, workforce training, access management policies, incident response, contingency planning
PhysicalFacility access controls, workstation security, device and media controls
TechnicalAccess control (unique user IDs, auto logoff, encryption), audit controls, integrity controls, transmission security
Critical Distinction — Required vs Addressable
Required: Must implement, no exceptions. Addressable: Must assess and implement if reasonable and appropriate — OR document why an equivalent alternative was used. Addressable does NOT mean optional. This trips up engineers in every HIPAA interview.

Risk Analysis — The #1 OCR Audit Finding

The single most important HIPAA concept. 45 CFR § 164.308(a)(1) requires you to:

  1. Identify the scope of ePHI across the organization
  2. Identify reasonably anticipated threats to ePHI
  3. Identify current vulnerabilities
  4. Assess likelihood and impact of each threat/vulnerability combination
  5. Determine the level of risk
  6. Implement security measures to reduce risk to a reasonable and appropriate level
  7. Document everything — and review/update periodically

Organizations that have been fined by OCR have most frequently cited failure to conduct a risk analysis as the root finding. It's not optional, it's not a one-time exercise, and it has to be documented.

Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with AWS

Before you put a single byte of ePHI in AWS, you must execute a BAA with AWS. It's available through the AWS Management Console. But the BAA only covers HIPAA-eligible services — not all AWS services qualify.

Interview Trap
Having a BAA with AWS does not mean all AWS services are in scope. You must verify each service you use against the AWS HIPAA Eligible Services Reference. Using a non-eligible service for ePHI workloads violates the BAA terms.

Breach Notification Rule & Timelines

Notification TargetTriggerDeadline
Affected IndividualsAny breach of unsecured PHIWithin 60 days of discovery
HHS (prominent)Breach affects 500+ individualsWithin 60 days of discovery
HHS (annual log)Breach affects fewer than 500By March 1 of following year

Safe Harbor — The Encryption Defense
If ePHI was encrypted with FIPS-validated algorithms and the keys were not compromised, it qualifies as "secured PHI" — and breach notification requirements may not apply. This is the Safe Harbor provision. It's the reason why encryption isn't just a nice-to-have in HIPAA; it's a legal shield.

Architecture

Enterprise AWS Architecture for PCI

Multi-Account Design with AWS Organizations

The gold standard for PCI in AWS is a multi-account, multi-OU architecture using AWS Organizations with Service Control Policies (SCPs) to enforce guardrails. This prevents PCI scope creep — the tendency for CDE connectivity to bleed across accounts.

# Recommended OU/Account Structure Root ├── Security OU │ ├── Security Tooling Account # GuardDuty delegated admin, Security Hub, Inspector │ └── Log Archive Account # Immutable CloudTrail, VPC Flow Logs, WAF logs ├── Infrastructure OU │ └── Network Account # Transit Gateway, AWS Network Firewall, egress ├── PCI OU ← SCPs enforce CDE guardrails here │ └── PCI Production Account # CDE — payment services, token vault interface ├── Non-PCI OU │ ├── Non-PCI Production Account │ └── Shared Services Account └── SDLC OU ├── Dev Account └── Staging Account

Hub-and-Spoke with Centralized Inspection

The CDE VPC uses a hub-and-spoke model via AWS Transit Gateway (TGW). The Network Account hosts the TGW and inspection layer. TGW route tables force all spoke-to-spoke traffic through the inspection VPC — this is called centralized inspection architecture.

Within the CDE VPC, enforce a three-tier subnet model:

Subnet TierContainsAccess Rule
Public SubnetALB / NLB onlyInternet → Load Balancer only. No EC2 with PAN access.
Private App SubnetApplication tier (EC2, ECS, Lambda)Can reach data subnet. Cannot reach internet directly.
Restricted Data SubnetRDS, DynamoDB, Secrets Manager endpointsOnly inbound from App Subnet on specific ports.
Interview: Security Groups vs NACLs
Security Groups are stateful — return traffic is automatically allowed. NACLs are stateless — you must explicitly allow both inbound AND outbound. NACLs operate at the subnet level; Security Groups at the instance level. Both are required — NACLs as defense-in-depth, Security Groups as primary micro-segmentation.

Architecture

Enterprise AWS Architecture for HIPAA

The HIPAA account structure mirrors PCI but the boundary is defined by ePHI data flows rather than card data flows. Key differences are in access control depth, the administrative controls requirement, and the breach response workflow.

Access Control: Least Privilege in Practice

HIPAA's Technical Safeguard for Access Control (45 CFR §164.312(a)(1)) requires unique user identification, emergency access procedures, automatic logoff, and encryption/decryption controls.

ControlAWS Implementation
Unique User IDsIAM Identity Center federated with IdP (Okta, Azure AD). No shared accounts.
No long-lived credentialsIAM Instance Roles and Execution Roles for workloads. No access keys on EC2.
No open SSH/RDPAWS Systems Manager Session Manager — agent-based, no open port 22, sessions logged to CloudWatch + S3.
Emergency access (break-glass)Dedicated break-glass IAM role with CloudWatch alarm on every use, requiring out-of-band authorization.
ABAC (Tag-based)IAM policies granting access based on tags (e.g., data-classification: ePHI). Scales better than RBAC at enterprise.

HIPAA Breach Response in AWS

PhaseAWS ToolAction
DetectionGuardDuty, MacieGuardDuty flags unauthorized access. Macie discovers ePHI in unexpected S3 locations.
InvestigationCloudTrail + Flow Logs + SIEMBuild forensic timeline. Document discovery timestamp — 60-day clock starts here.
ContainmentVPC Quarantine SGDedicated quarantine Security Group with no inbound/outbound. Isolate compromised EC2 without terminating for forensics.
DeterminationKMS audit logsConfirm whether encryption keys were compromised. If not → Safe Harbor may apply.
NotificationIncident ticketing60-day window to notify affected individuals and HHS (if 500+).

De-identification of PHI

De-identified data can leave the HIPAA boundary without restriction:

MethodHow It Works
Safe Harbor (18 Identifiers)Remove all 18 identifiers specified in the Privacy Rule: name, dates more specific than year, geographic data smaller than state, phone, email, SSN, MRN, and 11 more.
Expert DeterminationA qualified statistician certifies that re-identification risk is very low. More flexible but requires documented expert methodology.
AWS Tool for De-identification
Use Amazon Comprehend Medical to detect PHI in unstructured text for automated de-identification pipelines.
Both Frameworks

Shared AWS Services That Apply to Both

AWS Artifact

Self-service portal for AWS compliance documentation. For both PCI and HIPAA, pull: PCI DSS AOC (AWS's own QSA assessment), PCI Responsibility Summary (maps each requirement to AWS vs. customer responsibilities), SOC 1/2/3 Reports, and the HIPAA Whitepaper.

Critical Interview Point
AWS's AOC covers AWS's infrastructure, not your workloads. The shared responsibility model means you are responsible for your application's compliance. AWS's AOC is evidence that the platform is compliant — it's not a pass for your configuration.

AWS Config + Conformance Packs

AWS Config provides continuous compliance assessment. Pre-built Conformance Packs map Config rules to PCI DSS and HIPAA: Operational-Best-Practices-for-PCI-DSS and Operational-Best-Practices-for-HIPAA-Security. Pair with auto-remediation via SSM Automation documents to fix drift automatically (e.g., auto-enable S3 SSE if a bucket is created without it).

The engineers who stand out in compliance interviews aren't the ones who memorized requirement numbers. They're the ones who understand why the control exists and can describe how it maps to a real AWS component. When you explain PCI, lead with scoping and tokenization — that's where the real architecture thinking lives. When you explain HIPAA, lead with the Risk Analysis and the BAA — that's what separates a checkbox exercise from a defensible compliance program. Both frameworks converge on the same fundamentals: encrypt everything, log everything, control access tightly, and be able to prove it.
